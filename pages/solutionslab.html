02 — what's technically wrong

● six things google notices first.

None of these are catastrophic. Together they explain why mijnhemel.nl is hard to find, slow to load, and easy to dismiss on mobile.

seo

page architecture is too thin

  • only 3 pages indexed — homepage rendered twice (with and without trailing slash)
  • no dedicated pages for wine selection, café, team, or tastings
  • nothing for google to rank you for beyond "mijn hemel"
  • local search potential ("wijnbar hilversum") is wide open and unclaimed
brand

no logo, no recognizable mark

  • only a transparent png banner — not used as favicon, og image, or schema logo
  • typography: baskerville pulled in italic + 9 weights (massive overkill)
  • color palette is the wordpress block-editor default — six unrelated colors, no hierarchy
  • visual identity feels accidental, not intentional
social

no social previews

  • no og:image — link previews on whatsapp and instagram look broken
  • no twitter card metadata
  • only one social link (instagram) and it sits in the nav, not the footer
  • instagram (@gastrovinomijnhemel) is active but disconnected from the site
performance

slow on slow connections

  • elementor + wordpress = ~120kb of css before anything renders
  • baskerville with all italics + 9 weights = ~200kb of font payload
  • hero photo served at 2400px wide on every device
  • no lazy loading on below-the-fold images
conversion

nothing to do, nothing to book

  • no reservation form — only a phone number to remember
  • no online ordering for delivery — must call or email
  • no clear "book a tasting" path
  • visitors who are convinced have no way to act on it
structured data

invisible to google's local panel

  • no localbusiness or restaurant schema
  • opening hours scattered in plain text — google has to guess
  • address present but not marked up as postal address
  • map snippet, knowledge panel, and "open now" badge all forfeited

04 — top 5 priorities

● if you change only five things, change these.

  1. 01

    give each business its own page

    Right now the wijnwinkel, wijncafé and thuisbezorgd service share one paragraph. Each has a different audience, different intent, and different conversion goal. Splitting them roughly triples the surface area for google and clarifies the choice for visitors.

  2. 02

    add a real reservation form

    A wine café that's only bookable by phone leaves money on the table — especially for younger guests, group reservations, and out-of-hour intent. A simple form that emails to info@ closes the loop.

  3. 03

    add restaurant schema and an og image

    Two small pieces of html unlock the google local panel ("open now", map, hours, phone) and make every whatsapp share preview correctly. Both are missing today and both are free.

  4. 04

    describe the wines, name the people

    "Wijn uit alle windstreken" is poetry but not findable. List six wine families, name gerard and joop, photograph the room. This is the content that makes the rest of the marketing actually work.

  5. 05

    strip the wordpress weight, keep the voice

    The current site loads ~320kb of css and fonts before anything appears. A static build (vite, tailwind, two well-chosen fonts) cuts that by 80% — without losing one word of the existing copy.
